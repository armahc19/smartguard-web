## The scale of retail shrinkage

Retail shrinkage — losses from theft, fraud, and administrative errors — costs the global retail industry over $100 billion annually. In the UK alone, retailers lose an estimated £4.2 billion per year. Traditional loss prevention measures (uniformed guards, tagged merchandise, CCTV review) catch only a fraction of losses.

The challenge is compounded by the evolution of theft tactics. Organised retail crime groups use sophisticated methods to circumvent traditional security, and internal shrinkage (employee theft) remains notoriously difficult to detect with conventional approaches.

## An AI-first approach

AI-powered monitoring transforms existing CCTV cameras from passive recording devices into active loss prevention tools. By applying intelligent zone-based rules, retailers can detect suspicious behaviour patterns that would be invisible to human operators monitoring dozens of feeds simultaneously.

### Cashier zone rules

Define zones around each cashier station that monitor for specific patterns. Detect when a cashier area is active but no customer is present at the point of sale — a potential indicator of sweethearting (passing items without scanning). Alert when the cashier zone is occupied outside of scheduled shift hours.

More advanced rules can track the correlation between zone activity and transaction data, flagging periods of high foot traffic but low transaction volume for review.

### Aisle monitoring

Aisle zones track dwell time and movement patterns. While browsing is normal, certain patterns are associated with concealment: prolonged time in a specific section, particularly in aisles with high-value items. Zones with dwell-time rules can flag these patterns for real-time review.

Combine with occupancy counting to identify when aisles are empty — these are peak risk periods for concealment, as there are no natural observers present.

## After-hours protection

After-hours protection is perhaps the highest-ROI application of AI monitoring in retail. Time-based rules that activate when the store is closed can detect break-ins, internal after-hours access, and unauthorised early/late access by staff.

Unlike alarm systems that activate on door/window breaches, camera-based monitoring can detect presence anywhere in the store. An employee who stays behind after closing or enters before opening will be detected and flagged, even if they have legitimate access credentials.

## ROI case study

A mid-sized retailer with 15 stores deployed SmartGuard across their 10 highest-shrinkage locations. Within six months, they recorded a 23% reduction in total shrinkage across those stores. The system paid for itself within the first quarter through recovered losses alone.

Key results: after-hours incidents detected and prevented — 14 in six months. Sweethearting patterns identified — 8 cases leading to internal investigation. Average alert-to-response time — 45 seconds (from 12+ minutes with traditional CCTV review).
