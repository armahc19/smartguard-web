## What are virtual zones?

Virtual zones are software-defined regions drawn directly over a camera's field of view. Think of them as invisible tripwires and boundaries that transform a passive camera feed into an active monitoring system. When a person or object enters, exits, or lingers within a zone, rules can fire to trigger alerts, log events, or escalate to security teams.

Unlike physical barriers, virtual zones can be created, moved, and reconfigured in seconds. A loading bay zone during business hours can become an after-hours intrusion zone with a simple schedule change.

## Five essential zone patterns

After deploying SmartGuard across hundreds of sites, we've identified five zone patterns that deliver immediate value for virtually every operation. These patterns cover the most common security and operational monitoring scenarios.

### Entry/exit monitoring zones

Place narrow rectangular zones across doorways, gates, and entry points. These zones track directional movement — who's coming in and who's going out. Combined with time-based rules, they enable after-hours access alerts and footfall counting.

Best practice: make the zone wide enough to capture a full stride (about 1.5 metres in real-world distance) to avoid missed detections from fast-moving individuals.

### Restricted area zones

Draw zones around server rooms, stock rooms, hazardous material stores, or any area where unauthorised access should trigger an immediate alert. These zones typically use a simple rule: any person detected = alert.

For more nuanced control, combine with time schedules. A stock room might allow access during shift hours but trigger alerts outside of those windows.

### Occupancy counting zones

Cover open areas like retail floors, canteens, or waiting rooms with larger zones that count the number of people present. Set thresholds to alert when occupancy exceeds safe limits or when areas are unexpectedly empty.

Occupancy data also feeds into analytics dashboards, helping operations teams understand peak usage patterns and optimise staffing levels.

SmartGuard's zone editor lets you draw polygonal zones directly over your camera preview. Click to place vertices, double-click to close the shape. Each zone gets a name, colour, and set of rules. You can layer multiple zones on the same camera view and assign different rule sets to each.

Start with the five patterns above and expand as you learn how your space is actually used. The data from initial zones often reveals monitoring needs you hadn't anticipated.
