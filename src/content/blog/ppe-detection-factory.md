## The factory safety challenge

Manufacturing facilities face a persistent challenge: ensuring that every worker on the floor is wearing the correct personal protective equipment at all times. Manual spot-checks catch a fraction of violations, and safety officers can't be everywhere at once. A momentary lapse — removing a hard hat for comfort, forgetting safety glasses after a break — can result in serious injury.

Traditional approaches rely on a combination of training, signage, and periodic audits. While essential, these measures leave significant gaps in continuous compliance monitoring.

## AI-powered PPE detection

Computer vision models can be trained to identify specific PPE items — hard hats, high-visibility vests, safety glasses, gloves, and steel-capped boots — in real time. These models analyse camera feeds continuously, flagging any individual who enters a designated zone without the required equipment.

### What can be detected

Modern PPE detection models achieve over 95% accuracy for common items like hard hats and hi-vis vests. More specialised equipment (safety glasses, ear protection) requires additional training data but can reach similar accuracy levels with custom model fine-tuning.

SmartGuard's approach uses zone-specific PPE requirements. A welding area might require a full face shield and gloves, while an assembly line might only require safety glasses and steel-caps. This zone-based approach avoids false positives from areas with different requirements.

### Integration with alert systems

When a PPE violation is detected, the system can escalate through multiple channels simultaneously. A WhatsApp message to the floor supervisor, an email to the safety manager, and a logged event in the compliance dashboard — all within seconds of detection.

For critical zones (e.g., areas with molten materials or heavy machinery), alerts can integrate with access control systems to prevent entry until proper PPE is confirmed.

## Real-world results

One manufacturing plant in the Midlands deployed SmartGuard PPE detection across 12 cameras covering their main production floor and two loading bays. Within the first quarter, they recorded a 37% reduction in PPE-related safety incidents. Near-miss reports also decreased by 28%.

The key insight was that consistent, immediate feedback changed behaviour. Workers quickly learned that the system would catch violations, leading to a natural improvement in compliance without the adversarial dynamic that sometimes accompanies manual enforcement.

## Implementation steps

Deploying PPE detection follows a straightforward process. First, identify the zones where PPE is required and what specific equipment is needed in each area. Then, connect your existing camera feeds covering those zones to SmartGuard. Configure zone-specific PPE rules, set up your alert channels, and run a one-week calibration period to tune detection sensitivity.

During calibration, review flagged events to identify false positives (e.g., reflective surfaces being mistaken for hi-vis vests) and adjust zone boundaries or model sensitivity as needed.
