## The attention gap

Research from the Security Industry Authority shows that a human operator monitoring CCTV screens experiences significant attention degradation after just 20 minutes. In fact, studies indicate that up to 95% of security-relevant events go unnoticed during prolonged manual surveillance sessions. This isn't a training problem — it's a fundamental limitation of human attention spans.

Traditional security operations centres rely on teams rotating through camera feeds, but the sheer volume of visual data overwhelms even the most vigilant guards. A single retail location might have 30+ camera feeds running simultaneously. Multiply that across multiple sites, and you're looking at hundreds of simultaneous streams that no human team can effectively monitor.

## How AI monitoring works

AI-powered CCTV monitoring uses computer vision models trained to detect people, vehicles, objects, and behaviours in real time. Unlike human operators, these models maintain consistent attention across every camera feed, 24 hours a day, without fatigue or distraction.

### Real-time detection pipeline

SmartGuard's detection pipeline processes each camera frame through a multi-stage process. First, the frame is decoded and normalised. Then, a lightweight person-detection model identifies all individuals in the scene. Finally, the detected positions are compared against configured virtual zones to determine whether any rules have been triggered.

This entire process happens in under 200 milliseconds per frame, enabling true real-time alerting. When a rule fires, notifications are dispatched via WhatsApp, email, or webhook within seconds — not minutes.

### Zone-based logic

Virtual zones transform static camera feeds into intelligent monitoring surfaces. You draw polygons over camera views to define areas of interest — restricted zones, entry points, loading bays, or cashier areas. Each zone can have its own set of rules: occupancy thresholds, time-based access controls, or loitering detection.

This approach means a single camera can serve multiple security functions simultaneously, something that would require multiple dedicated guards in a traditional setup.

## Measurable outcomes

Organisations deploying AI CCTV monitoring typically report a 60-80% reduction in security incidents that go undetected. Response times drop from an average of 15 minutes (human detection + escalation) to under 30 seconds. Insurance providers are increasingly offering premium discounts for sites with verified AI monitoring in place.

Beyond security, the data collected enables operational insights. Footfall patterns, peak occupancy times, and zone utilisation metrics help operators optimise staffing, layout, and procedures.
